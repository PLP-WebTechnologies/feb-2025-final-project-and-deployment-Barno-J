A Mental Health & Public Health View

Today, mental health is no longer a luxury but a necessity. As a Public Health Officer, I have seen firsthand how mental well-being is deeply connected to all other aspects of being. This blog is a space to explore those connections, share real stories, and offer actionable insights that empower individuals and communities.

About Me

Hi, my name is Barno June, a Public Health Officer passionate about improving lives improving beyond the clinical setting.

I started this blog because often, people are silently battling depression, anxiety, or trauma, and unable to access help because they can't afford it, or because services simply aren't there.

A few years ago, while working in a rural health facility, I met a teenage boy named David (name changed for privacy). His school performance had plummeted, he was often isolated, and his mother brought him in thinking it was a physical illness. But what he really needed was mental health support, one that was not recognized and did not exist nearby. That encounter made it painfully clear that mental health approach should be treated like a real public health issue and that things needed to change.

This blog is my way of continuing that mission. I want to help bring awareness, provide useful information, and push for a society where mental wellness is accessible to all, no matter where they live or how much they earn.

Tips to Maintain Mental Health

Daily Habits and Routines

  • Stick to sleep schedules
  • Limit media use
  • Take short breaks during your workday
  • Engage in daily physical activity, even a walk

Community & Social Support

  • Join a support group or community initiative
  • Reach out to family or friends regularly
  • Volunteer: helping others boosts your own well-being
  • Talk rather than bottle up what you feel

Accessing Care and Knowing Your Rights

  • Know where your nearest mental health services are
  • Seek support early before crisis
  • If therapy is expensive, explore community health centers or NGOs
  • Advocate for policies that increase mental health funding and reduce stigma
